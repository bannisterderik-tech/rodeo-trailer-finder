The vision

Season planning shouldn't feel like a rodeo of its own.

We're building the one place haulers open when the season kicks off — and keep open all the way to the last go-round.

Every hauler knows the drill: a barrel race three states over, and suddenly you're cross-checking arena clearances, calling around for horse-friendly lodging, hunting a feed store that's still open, and praying the only bridge on the route isn't 12′6″.

Haulin' Hooves pulls all of it into one rig-aware map. Tell us your trailer once — gooseneck or bumper-pull, length, height, weight, living quarters — and every arena, stay, feed store, fuel stop, farrier, vet and dump station is filtered to what actually works for your haul.

  • One rig profile that powers fit checks everywhere
  • Routes that auto-drop fuel, feed, dumps & emergency vets
  • "Last verified" stamps so you're not calling dead numbers
"Where can I actually park this thing, sleep my horses, and get back on the road — without ten browser tabs and a bad hunch?"
That's the question Haulin' Hooves is built to answer. This prototype is our first swing at it — real vision, sample data, and a wide-open ear for what you'd need to make it a daily driver.
What's inside

Everything the season throws at you — handled.

A working prototype you can tap through today. Every screen below is live in the demo.

Trail map

Arenas, lodging, feed, parking, farriers, vets and dumps on one continental map — every layer filtered to stops that fit your rig.

Real fit engine

Bridge clearance, length, grade and turning checks per rig — surfaced as a plain-English road verdict and a crosswind / brake-fade / tight-turn risk profile.

Route mode

Enter your address and first event; the planner builds the corridor and auto-drops the nearest fuel, dump, feed and 24/7 vet on every leg — flagging roads that block your rig.

Lodging & stops

Horse-friendly stays, feed stores, fuel and parking with the details that matter: pull-through access, hookups, clearances and hours.

Vets & dumps

24/7 emergency vets and dump stations mapped alongside everything else — plus an SOS that calls the nearest vet when it counts.

Reviews & freshness

Star ratings from other haulers and a "last verified" stamp on every listing, so you trust what you're driving toward.

Trail styles

Six western color skins — Sunset, Rosé, Turquoise, Sage, Denim, Buckskin — that retint the whole app, right down to the 3D rig.

Multiple rigs & 3D setup

Build your trailer in an interactive 3D configurator, save several rigs, and switch between them — fit recomputes everywhere instantly.

Deep actions

Tap to call or text farriers, feed stores and emergency vets, reserve stays, hold parking, and add events straight to your calendar.

How it works

Three steps to a season that runs itself.

1

Build your rig

Gooseneck or bumper-pull, length, height, loaded weight, horse slots, living quarters. Do it once in the 3D setup.

2

See what fits

The map and season feed light up with fit badges — fits, tight, or too big — for every arena, stay and stop.

3

Plan the haul

Drop in your start and first event; get a corridor with fuel, feed, dumps and emergency vets on every leg.

Meet the founder

Kimberly Karlovich

Horse lover · Hauler · Founder

Kimberly has spent a lifetime around horses — and a whole lot of it behind the wheel of a loaded trailer, chasing events across state lines. She's lived every headache Haulin' Hooves is built to fix: the last-minute lodging scramble, the feed store that closed at five, the clearance you find out about the hard way.

She started Haulin' Hooves with one belief — that the people who love this life shouldn't lose half of it to logistics. Her mission is simple: one trusted app for the entire horse-event season, built with the community it serves, so haulers can spend less time planning and more time riding.

This prototype is the first mile of that ride. It's here to show the vision and, just as importantly, to listen — so the people who'll actually use it get to shape it.
